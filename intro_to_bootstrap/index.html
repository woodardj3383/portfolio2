About Me:

Title Description August 11, 2020

I moved to Chicago in 2017 and within a year purchased a classic Chicago three-flat, brick building in McKinley Park on the southwest side, fulfilling the role of both homeowner and landlord. Originally from the Alabama Gulf Coast, I pursued an MA in American Studies at the University of Alabama and a Phd at Saint Louis University. While in academia, I taught and assited with numerous undergraduate courses. My concentration in American Urban History was a motivating factor for moving to Chicago.

In adition to my studies, I also have about 8 years experience as a bartender. Such a profession is filled with high-times and fond memories, but I was already transitioning out of that profession when the pandemic struck in March of 2020. In my leisure time, I like discovering new areas of the city on my bike, seeing live rock shows, and indulging in Chicago's many bars and eateries (pandemic permitting).

Whether it be teaching, bartending, or any of my other odd jobs, my profeesional goals have always hinged on my skills as a people-person. While this is all well and good I am looking for a new challenge and a new direction in my professional endeavors. I am excited to enter the world of programming because of its basis in logic and hard science. This portfolio will showcase what I have and will learn in the coming years and will hopefully be a testament to all of the work I have put into this venture.
